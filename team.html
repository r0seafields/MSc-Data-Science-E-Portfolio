Team Exercise Outcomes

This page showcases the outcomes of group-based assignments and collaborative work from Unit 6 of the Deciphering Big Data module.

Project Summary

Our team worked collaboratively to design a secure, scalable, and regulation-compliant database system for MedicaidUK, a fictitious private healthcare organisation. Each member contributed to the planning, modelling, and documentation of the database schema, ensuring alignment with GDPR, NHS standards, and organisational requirements.

Team Contributions

  • Database Design: Collaborative entity-relationship (ER) modelling and schema normalisation.
  • Compliance & Regulation: Ensured adherence to GDPR and NHS legal requirements.
  • Technical Evaluation: Compared relational and non-relational architectures to identify best fit.
  • Documentation: Drafted executive summaries and reports to communicate findings to non-technical stakeholders.
  • Collaboration: Regular check-ins via Zoom and Google Docs ensured transparency and efficient progress.

Key Learnings

Working in a team allowed us to leverage diverse skills and perspectives. I developed stronger collaborative, communication, and critical thinking skills while contributing to a technically robust database solution. The experience highlighted the importance of aligning technical work with stakeholder requirements and the value of peer feedback in refining designs.

Reflections

This project reinforced my ability to work in a team, coordinate tasks effectively, and translate complex technical concepts into actionable solutions. Our outputs demonstrated both individual accountability and collective achievement.
