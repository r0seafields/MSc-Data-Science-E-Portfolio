Unit 9 – Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

This unit built on the Artificial Neural Networks introduced in Unit 7 by exploring Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), which are specifically designed for image recognition and computer vision tasks. Through the practical notebook activity, I investigated how CNNs automatically identify and learn important features within images before classifying them. This demonstrated how deep learning techniques can solve complex visual recognition problems that would be difficult to achieve using traditional machine learning approaches.

The practical exercise helped me understand how convolutional layers, pooling layers and fully connected layers work together to recognise increasingly complex image features. Although the mathematical concepts behind CNNs are challenging, completing the notebook allowed me to appreciate how these networks are trained and why they have become the standard approach for image classification tasks.

Reflection

I found this unit particularly interesting because it demonstrated how Artificial Intelligence can analyse images in ways that closely resemble human visual recognition. Building on the neural networks studied in Unit 7, I was able to see how CNNs extend these ideas by automatically identifying important image features without requiring manual feature selection.

Initially, I found the different layers within a CNN difficult to understand, particularly how convolution and pooling contribute to the final classification. However, working through the notebook helped me visualise the learning process and appreciate how each layer extracts progressively more detailed information from an image. This practical experience reinforced my understanding of how deep learning models are applied to real-world computer vision problems.

Looking back, this unit strengthened my appreciation of how rapidly Artificial Intelligence has developed and why Convolutional Neural Networks are now widely used in applications such as medical imaging, autonomous vehicles, facial recognition and object detection. While I recognise that I still need to develop a deeper understanding of the underlying mathematics, I now have a solid conceptual understanding of how CNNs operate and why they are so effective for image recognition.

Article Review

As part of this unit, I reviewed a BBC News article discussing the growing use of Convolutional Neural Networks and facial recognition technology. The article explained how CNNs have the potential to improve efficiency and accuracy by analysing large amounts of visual information much faster than humans.

I believe this technology offers significant benefits across a range of industries, including healthcare, security and transport, where rapid image analysis can improve decision-making and make services safer and more reliable. However, it also raises several important ethical concerns that need careful consideration.

One concern is privacy. If facial recognition technology is used for surveillance, people could be monitored continuously without their knowledge or consent. There is also the risk of bias if CNN models are trained using datasets that do not fairly represent all groups of people, potentially leading to unfair or discriminatory decisions. In addition, increasing reliance on Artificial Intelligence may reduce the need for some jobs, creating wider social and economic challenges.

Overall, I believe that while Convolutional Neural Networks provide considerable benefits, they should be implemented responsibly with appropriate regulation, transparency and human oversight. Ensuring that AI systems are fair, accurate and respectful of individual rights is essential if society is to gain the full benefits of this technology.
