Unit 9 – Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

Machine Learning Module

Overview

This unit explored Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), a specialised type of Artificial Neural Network designed for image recognition and computer vision. Through practical experimentation and an article review, I investigated how CNNs learn image features and considered the wider ethical implications of facial recognition technology.

Practical Activity

I completed a practical notebook exploring the implementation of a Convolutional Neural Network for image classification. The exercise demonstrated how convolutional, pooling and fully connected layers work together to recognise patterns within images.

Reflection

This unit expanded on the neural network concepts introduced in Unit 7 by demonstrating how CNNs automatically extract important image features before performing classification. Initially, I found the different network layers difficult to understand, particularly the role of convolution and pooling. However, completing the notebook helped me appreciate how these layers work together to identify increasingly complex features within an image.

The activity strengthened my understanding of deep learning and showed why CNNs have become the standard approach for computer vision tasks such as medical imaging, autonomous vehicles and object recognition. Although the underlying mathematics remains challenging, I now have a much stronger conceptual understanding of how CNNs are trained and applied to real-world problems.

Article Review

As part of this unit, I reviewed a BBC News article discussing facial recognition technology powered by Convolutional Neural Networks. The article highlighted how CNNs can improve efficiency and accuracy by analysing images far more quickly than humans, particularly in areas such as healthcare, transport and security.

While these technologies offer significant benefits, they also raise important ethical concerns. Facial recognition systems may threaten privacy through widespread surveillance, while biased training data can result in unfair or discriminatory decisions. In addition, increased automation may affect employment in some sectors.

Overall, I believe CNNs provide considerable benefits when implemented responsibly. Appropriate regulation, transparency and human oversight are essential to ensure that Artificial Intelligence systems remain accurate, fair and respectful of individual rights.
