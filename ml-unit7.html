Unit 7 – Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs)

This unit introduced Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) and explored how they can be used to solve classification problems. Through practical notebook activities, I learned how perceptrons make decisions, how weights influence predictions, and how multi-layer perceptrons use activation functions to recognise more complex patterns within data.

This was one of the most interesting units of the module because it felt like a step beyond traditional statistical methods and into modern Artificial Intelligence. The practical exercises demonstrated how simple processing units (neurons) can be connected together to learn from data and improve predictions, providing a foundation for more advanced deep learning techniques.

Reflection

I found this unit particularly engaging because it introduced concepts that felt much closer to the Artificial Intelligence systems we hear about today. While I had previously worked with statistical methods such as regression and correlation, neural networks represented a completely different way of approaching prediction by allowing computers to learn patterns from data rather than relying on predefined rules.

The perceptron exercise helped me understand how a single artificial neuron can perform simple decision-making tasks, such as implementing the logical AND operator. Although the code itself was relatively straightforward, it demonstrated the importance of weights and thresholds in determining the final output. This provided a solid foundation before moving on to more advanced neural network structures.

Building on this, the multi-layer perceptron activity introduced hidden layers and sigmoid activation functions. Initially, I found it difficult to understand why additional layers were needed, but seeing how they enabled the network to model more complex relationships helped the theory make much more sense. Running the notebook and observing how the outputs changed gave me a better appreciation of how neural networks gradually learn from data during training.

Looking back, this was one of my favourite units because it introduced concepts that I had often heard about but never fully understood. It increased my confidence with neural networks and sparked my interest in deep learning, particularly as a foundation for convolutional neural networks and other modern AI techniques explored later in the module.
