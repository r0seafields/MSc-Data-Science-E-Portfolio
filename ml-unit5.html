Unit 5 – Jaccard Distance/Dissimilarity

This unit focused on understanding the Jaccard coefficient and how it is used to measure similarity between observations containing binary data. The activity involved calculating the Jaccard similarity and Jaccard distance between three individuals using pathological test results before interpreting what the results represented.

Reflection

Unlike correlation or regression, which I had previously encountered during my BSc Psychology degree, the Jaccard coefficient was completely new to me. I initially found it one of the more difficult topics in the module because I struggled to understand exactly what the calculation was measuring and why shared absences were ignored. At first, the formula seemed quite abstract and I found it difficult to connect the mathematics with its practical application.
