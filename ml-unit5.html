Unit 5 – Jaccard Distance/Dissimilarity

Machine Learning Module

Overview

This unit introduced the Jaccard coefficient, a similarity measure commonly used with binary data. The practical activity involved calculating Jaccard similarity and Jaccard distance between individuals based on pathological test results and interpreting the meaning of the calculated values. This demonstrated how similarity measures can be applied within machine learning and healthcare datasets.

Practical Activity

I completed the Jaccard Distance exercise by manually calculating the Jaccard similarity coefficient and Jaccard distance for three individuals. The activity involved interpreting binary data and comparing the similarity between different observations.

Reflection

Unlike correlation and regression, which I had previously encountered during my BSc Psychology degree, the Jaccard coefficient was a completely new concept. I initially found it challenging because I struggled to understand why shared absences were ignored when measuring similarity. Working through the calculations step-by-step helped me understand that the metric focuses only on shared positive characteristics, making it particularly useful when analysing binary datasets.

Completing the exercise helped me appreciate the importance of selecting an appropriate similarity measure depending on the type of data being analysed. I also recognised how Jaccard similarity is widely used in healthcare, bioinformatics and information retrieval, where identifying common attributes is often more meaningful than matching negative values. This activity strengthened my confidence in interpreting mathematical similarity measures and applying them to real-world machine learning problems.

Summary

This activity expanded my understanding of similarity measures used within machine learning and demonstrated how binary data can be analysed using the Jaccard coefficient. It strengthened my mathematical reasoning and provided valuable insight into how similarity metrics are applied within healthcare and other data-driven fields.
